Design Process Details

Brief Summary:

TOUCH community services is a non-profit organisation in Singapore that was registered in 1992.

This app was created to allow users to easily look for upcoming or ongoing TOUCH community services and events.

Users can use the app to sign-up or stay updated without having to search for TOUCH's website all the time.

With this app, users can turn on push notifications which will allow them to get notified when there are upcoming events or when events begin.

The app also allows user to log in using their Singpass which helps with efficiency.

Kinesthetic and Haptic functions were also used in the creation of this app.

Kinesthetic functions were used to track progress of users for certain events/workshops that may require users to move around or hit daily goals (Step goals etc.)

Haptic functions were used to notify users when there are new notifications on upcoming or ongoing events (Phone vibrates when there are new notifcations)

Key Demographic:

The app can be used by audiences of any age. However, it is mainly focussed on accessibility for the elderly.

The target audience would mainly be Singaporeans as TOUCH community services is a Singapore-based organisation that mainly organises events in Singapore.

The app allows the elderly to stay engaged in the community and participate in local events and workshops, which may help the elderly enhance their social connections.

By creating an app, it will also help the elderly to embrace technology and leverage its benefits to improve their community involvement.

Color Scheme:

Analogous.

Touch uses mainly red and white in their designs so I decided to use colours ranging from orange to red.

#FF7474 and #FFA700 was used to create a gradient for the app's background.

#FFFA7E was used for the buttons on the app and it was also used as a stroke color for ovals to outline sections for the guided tour.

#FF9300 was used for settings, support, donations, and events buttons.

#FF4343 was used for the "Extract information from Singpass" buttons.

#FFB560 was used for the field boxes in forms.

#FFFFFF was used for the background for certain images and text box fill for guided tour texts.

#000000 was used as a text color.
