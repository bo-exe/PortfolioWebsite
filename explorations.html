Render - Jett

Design Process Details

Brief Summary:

This project was created as a way for me to practice posing and lighting using blender.

For this project, I had used a pre-made blender pack of character models from a game called VALORANT.

Using the pack, I played around with the model, shifiting and rotating certain bones of the model to create a pose that I liked.

Eventhough the process was tougher at the beginning, I eventually got the hang of it and I decided to add a few point lights to make certain parts glow.

I also decided that I would export the model and use Photoshop to add details such as a red-ish background.

With that in mind, I added a few point lights such as red lights behind the character to make it seem light the light is being emitted by the background.

From there, I exported the model and added the background, smoke effects, texts that says the character's name (Jett) in both English and Korean.

In order to include more aspects of the game that inspired this project into the final work, I added 4 icons which are icons of the character's abilities in the game.

Tools used:

Adobe - Photoshop

Blender

Render - Killjoy

Design Process Details

Brief Summary:

Just like the Render - Jett Project, this project was another way for me to learn to pose using Blender.

Using a different character model from the same model pack, I played around and looked for an ideal pose with the end result including the background and details in mind.

Since I had done this after the Render - Jett projext, I was more used to certain stuff and it did not take as long as it did with the first project.

For this character, the two colors that I felt represented the character well that really stoof out for me were yellow and purple and thus I decided to focus on those colors.

I knew I wanted the character's name to be behind the character and I thought it would be a great idea to do a half and half design using yellow and purple texts.

Thus, I had used a yellow point light on the left and a purple point light on the right to make it seem like the texts are emitting light.

After exporting the render and going to photoshop, I looked for a background image that fits the theme. I then added the texts with half and half design as I planned.

I added some duplicated texts and overlayed them with lower opacity to give the text appear more 3D.

I also cut the next of the character's name in half and added them to the top and the bottom with lines on the sides to create a border around the main subject.

Tools used:

Adobe - Photoshop

Blender

Render - LOCK//IN || Raze

Design Process Details

Brief Summary:

For this project, my main goal was to find ways to make little objects related to the main subject appear more natural using posing.

The start of this project was the same as every other projects.

I used a character model and played around with poses to find an ideal pose before importing models of extra content such as the knife, poison and grenade.

Using blender, I positioned the different models of character abilities in a way I thought would look more realistic.

Since I wanted the grenade to look like it was thrown upwards by the main character and the knife and poison to look like they were thrown from behind the character, I had made the grenade closer to the camera.

Since I wanted to grenade to emit smoke, I decided to pull the pin out of the model to look like the pin had been removed and the grenade is in effect.

I also added white point lights behind the character as I knew I would habe white texts and designs behind the character and I wanted it to look like the texts were emiting light.

I also added lights of various colors based on the different objects such as orange for the grenade, and green for the poison to make them stand out a little more.

Then, the process is the same in photoshop where I added texts and extra designs. I also added smoke effects and trails to the abilities to make them look more realistic.

Tools used:

Adobe - Photoshop

Blender
